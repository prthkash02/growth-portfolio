# Sources: Cold Outreach for B2B SaaS

Quick note on what this is. I picked cold outreach for B2B SaaS, but I looked at it through an AI lens: where is AI actually changing how people prospect, and where is it just adding more noise to everyone's already-full inbox?

I went with this topic because I've done sales. That matters here. I can read an outreach tip and tell pretty fast whether it's something that would actually work or whether it just sounds clever on LinkedIn. The brief said quality over volume, so I didn't try to rack up 50 names. I found 10 people who do this for a living and went deep.

How I picked them:

- They're operators, not commentators. Every one has built an SDR team, run an agency, or built the software. They're not just posting takes.
- I wanted a mix of formats (podcasts, LinkedIn, YouTube, newsletters) so the research isn't one-note.
- And I picked people who disagree with each other on purpose. About half are in the "be human, stop spamming" camp (Braun, Venetz, Holland). The other half are the "use AI to personalize at scale" camp (Allred, Coleman, Mahrle). The whole point of this project, for me, is sitting in that disagreement. My take, which the job description actually phrases well, is: AI as a superpower, not a crutch.

### 1. Josh Braun
Josh is the first person I'd hand a new rep. He built the "Poke the Bear" approach, and his whole thing is starting conversations without the desperate "commission breath" that makes buyers shut down. Of everyone here, he's the clearest on *why* most cold outreach gets ignored in the first place. He anchors the human side of this for me.
- What I grabbed: 2 of his videos (cold email + a full cold-call/objection script) and 2 LinkedIn posts.
- [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/josh-braun/) · [joshbraun.com](https://joshbraun.com/)

### 2. Jason Bay
Jason runs Outbound Squad and has trained something like 20,000 reps at companies like Shopify, Gong, and Zoom. What I like about him is that everything is a repeatable framework, which is exactly what you need if you're trying to write a playbook and not just collect clever one-liners. His podcast is 400+ episodes deep.
- What I grabbed: a long cold-call/objection training transcript (~2,800 lines) and 3 LinkedIn posts.
- [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jasondbay) · [Podcast](https://outboundsquad.com/podcast/)

### 3. Will Allred

He posts live teardowns of real emails. He's one of my anchors on the AI-at-scale side, though interestingly he's also the one who'll tell you when personalization isn't worth the effort.
- What I grabbed: 3 LinkedIn posts (incl. his "founder card" play and a sharp one on when personalization *doesn't* pay off).
- [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/williamallred/)

### 4. Jed Mahrle
Jed scaled the SDR org at PandaDoc and now runs Practical Prospecting, doing done-for-you cold email and LinkedIn automation. He sits right on the line between human craft and automation, which is the exact tension I care about here. His newsletter is paywalled, so I leaned on his LinkedIn for the substance.
- What I grabbed: 3 LinkedIn posts (his full modern-prospecting framework, a good one on tone over scripts, and a LinkedIn prospecting trick). Newsletter previews are noted in `other/newsletters`.

Kyle is the best I found at explaining how to use AI to *research and personalize* outbound at scale without sounding like a robot. He was an early Looker employee, ran SDR/growth at Clari through ~10x ARR, was CMO at Copy.ai, and is now VP Marketing at ClickUp.

### 9. Thibaut Souyris
Thibaut is the creative-outreach guy. Video, LinkedIn voice notes, the formats most reps are too nervous to try, and he reports strong reply and meeting rates from them. I kept him in partly as a counterweight: he's proof that some of what works is exactly the stuff AI can't fully copy.

### 10. Becc Holland
Becc runs Flip the Script, and honestly her one idea unlocked this whole project for me: she separates *personalization* (1:1) from *relevance* (1:many). Once you look at outreach that way, the AI debate basically answers itself, because it tells you what AI should and shouldn't be automating.
- What I grabbed: 2 video transcripts (a personalized sequence build + an email teardown) and 1 LinkedIn post on what actually breaks a buyer out of "do nothing."
- [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/beccholland-flipthescript/) · [flipthescript.com](https://www.flipthescript.com/)

## The disagreement I'm trying to resolve

| "Be human" camp | "AI at scale" camp |
|----------------------|------------------|
| Braun, Venetz, Holland, Souyris | Allred, Coleman, Mahrle, Tatulea |
| Earn the right. Relevance beats volume. | AI lets you research and personalize at volume. |

The way I'd reconcile it comes straight from Becc Holland. AI is genuinely great at *relevance at scale* (firmographic, technographic, trigger-based). Humans still win on *true 1:1 personalization* for the accounts big enough to justify the time. That's the spine of the playbook I'd build out of this, and I dig into it more in [`synthesis.md`](synthesis.md).
