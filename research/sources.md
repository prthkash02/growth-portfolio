# Sources: Cold Outreach for B2B SaaS

The 10 practitioners this research is built on, with links, content dates, and short annotations. Posts and transcripts for each person live in [`linkedin-posts/`](linkedin-posts), [`youtube-transcripts/`](youtube-transcripts), and [`other/`](other). My point of view on all of it is in [`synthesis.md`](synthesis.md).

**Why this topic.** I come from a sales background, so I can actually judge whether outreach advice is good or just loud. That felt like the point of the exercise. I framed it around AI ("where is AI genuinely changing cold outreach, and where is it just adding noise?") to fit the AI-native role.

**How I picked these 10.** Three filters:

1. Operators, not commentators. Every person here has built an SDR team, run an agency, or built the software. None are pure pundits.
2. A spread of formats (podcasts, YouTube, newsletters, LinkedIn) so the research has range.
3. People who disagree with each other on purpose. Roughly half preach "be human, stop spamming," the other half preach "use AI to personalize at scale." Sitting in that disagreement is the whole project.

## The experts in detail

### 1. Josh Braun
The first person I'd hand a new rep. He built the "Poke the Bear" approach, and his whole thing is starting conversations without the desperate "commission breath" that makes buyers shut down. Of everyone here, he's clearest on *why* most cold outreach gets ignored. He anchors the human side of this for me.
- Collected: 2 videos (cold email + a full cold-call/objection script) and 2 LinkedIn posts. See [posts](linkedin-posts/josh-braun.md) and [transcripts](youtube-transcripts/josh-braun).
- Links: [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/josh-braun/), [joshbraun.com](https://joshbraun.com/)

### 2. Jason Bay
Runs Outbound Squad and has trained something like 20,000 reps at companies like Shopify, Gong, and Zoom. What I like is that everything is a repeatable framework, which is exactly what you need to write a playbook and not just collect one-liners.
- Collected: a long cold-call/objection training transcript (~2,800 lines) and 3 LinkedIn posts. See [posts](linkedin-posts/jason-bay.md) and [transcript](youtube-transcripts/jason-bay).
- Links: [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jasondbay), [Podcast](https://outboundsquad.com/podcast/)

### 3. Will Allred
Co-founded Lavender, the AI email-coaching tool built on 2B+ analyzed emails, so he has a data view of cold email almost nobody else has. He's an anchor on the AI-at-scale side, though he's also the one who will tell you when personalization is not worth the effort.
- Collected: 3 LinkedIn posts (incl. his "founder card" play and a sharp one on when personalization does not pay off). See [posts](linkedin-posts/will-allred.md).
- Links: [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/williamallred/)

- Collected: 3 LinkedIn posts (full modern-prospecting framework, tone over scripts, a LinkedIn prospecting trick). His newsletter is paywalled, so the substance is in the posts. See [posts](linkedin-posts/jed-mahrle.md).

Runs Flip the Script, and her one idea unlocked this whole project for me: she separates *personalization* (1:1) from *relevance* (1:many). Once you look at outreach that way, the AI debate basically answers itself.

## The disagreement I set out to resolve

| "Be human" camp | "AI at scale" camp |
|-----------------|--------------------|
| Braun, Venetz, Holland, Souyris | Allred, Coleman, Mahrle, Tatulea |
| Earn the right. Relevance beats volume. | AI lets you research and personalize at volume. |

The bridge comes straight from Becc Holland. AI is genuinely great at *relevance at scale* (firmographic, technographic, trigger-based). Humans still win on *true 1:1 personalization* for the accounts big enough to justify the time. That tiered idea is the spine of the playbook, and I work it out in full in [`synthesis.md`](synthesis.md).
