## 2. The only 2 ways to succeed at outbound right now
**Posted:** ~May 2026 · [permalink](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jasondbay_there-are-only-2-ways-to-succeed-at-outbound-ugcPost-7457772290135445504-wat6)

**Way 1 — Be disproportionately better than everyone else.** The old outbound math is broken:
- 150 calls/week × 5% pick-up = 7–8 conversations → 10% booking → <1 meeting → ~2–3 meetings/month → <1 new opp/month.
- A **top-10% cold caller converts ~30%** of live conversations → 2–3 meetings booked → 7–9 meetings/month → ~2–3 new opps/month.
- Analogy: outbound is like online dating — the top 10% get 60%+ of matches; the bottom 60% get 4%. To make cold calling work, **be top 10–20% on skill.**

**Way 2 — Take the path of less resistance ("inbound-led outbound").** Marketing creates demand; sales captures it. Tactics: events/dinners, good webinars, world-class content/thought leadership, co-creating content with prospects (e.g. podcast guests), influencer partnerships, case studies with decision-makers, signal/trigger-based prospecting, leveraging execs' networks, strong offers. Downside: it's a multi-quarter / year-to-ROI play — "but this IS the moat. Your reach/brand/distribution are the moat."

## 3. Disqualification — the most underrated outbound strategy
**Posted:** ~May 2026 · [permalink](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jasondbay_disqualification-the-single-most-important-ugcPost-7457362362195652608-irIv)

It takes ~**12–15 outbound touches** (email/phone/social) to reach a buyer — so you should be disqualifying the *majority* of accounts. Case: a cybersecurity client was chasing ~12 verticals and 4–5 personas. They pulled their data, found most pipeline came from **manufacturing + healthcare** and meetings with **heads/directors of security + plant managers**, refocused the SDRs there — and **qualified meetings rose 22%** over 90 days.

How to apply:
1. **Pull closed-won & closed-lost data** (volume scaled to your segment).
2. **Identify patterns** — who's easiest to sell to (win rate, deal size, sales cycle), who gets best results (case studies/testimonials), shared traits (industry, size, tech stack, geo, funding, triggers).
3. **Narrow segmentation** — by niche, size, trigger, or a combination.

> "Disqualification is an ATTITUDE. Don't waste time on poor-fit accounts… use AI to help with this exercise."
