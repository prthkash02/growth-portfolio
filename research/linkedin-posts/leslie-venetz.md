## 1. A 3-step cold email framework
**Posted:** ~2024 · [permalink](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/leslievenetz_coldoutreach-coldemail-outboundsales-share-7207373797245546496-FH4R)

A 3-step cold email framework you can start using today:
1. Share a non-obvious insight that you believe is relevant to the prospect.
2. Validate that you got it right.
3. Leverage the Voice of the Customer to build social proof.

✨ **Value > Validation > Trust** ✨

Reminder: you have something you can teach your prospects (a non-obvious insight) — even if you're new on the job, even if you don't know the industry that well, even if you're not in a closing role. As sellers, we talk to our ICP (our prospect's peer group) WAY more than they do:
- We learn **trends** → the challenges you hear prospects talk about over and over.
- We learn **strategy** → the big bets they're making in the coming months.
- We learn **insider tips** → the best events they attend, tech they use, articles they've read.

You have more to offer than you realize. What's a non-obvious insight you can share today?

## 2. 3 favorite cold email subject lines
**Posted:** ~late 2025 · [permalink](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/leslievenetz_3-favorite-subject-lines-as-a-cold-email-ugcPost-7397302840404295680-n9ex)

3 favorite subject lines as a cold email strategist that audits 100s of emails a year:
1. **"Thank You"** — followed by a genuine reason you feel thankful. (And don't use "game changer.")
2. **"[Publication]: [Topic]"** — e.g. *Reuters: Resilience* or *WSJ: Supplements*. Use a widely known, reputable publication. A great way to provide value even if your org doesn't have much content. When you share value, don't just send an article with "thought you'd like this" — tell them exactly why it's worth their time.
3. **"[Referred By Name]"** — using a colleague/peer/shared connection who referred you is GOLD. Never lie.

## 3. 5 mistakes outbound sellers make every week
**Posted:** ~2025 · [permalink](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/leslievenetz_5-mistakes-i-see-outbound-sellers-make-every-share-7318261961438216193-k1ZM)

1. **Writing like a marketer instead of a seller** — cold emails aren't blog posts; you're starting a conversation, not building brand.
2. **Lazy follow-ups like "just checking in"** — you're not checking in, you're hoping they magically became interested.
3. **Avoiding the phone entirely** — 100 emails a day but no calls isn't outbound.
4. **Treating LinkedIn like a billboard** — talking *at* people isn't selling; engage in comments and DMs.
5. **Gimmicks like "quick question" / "got 15 mins?"** — buyers see through it; without value up front, the answer is always no.

> "Outbound in 2025 demands that you rise above silver bullets because there are none. You need a strategy that prioritizes relevance, repetition, and real conversations."

## 4. Why she double- (or triple-) taps to open a sequence
**Posted:** ~2024 · [permalink](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/leslievenetz_salessequences-outboundsales-coldoutreach-ugcPost-7230566745411874818-mKND)

(Caveat: she no longer cold-calls twice in a row — doesn't want to make people anxious.) Otherwise, double-tapping is a key sequencing strategy:
1. **Enhanced recall** — the first touch may not stick; rapid repetition solidifies your presence.
2. **Persistence without pressure** — a quick second touch signals serious-but-not-aggressive.
3. **Multi-channel engagement** — vary the channel (email → LinkedIn → call); each reinforces the others.
4. **Sense of urgency** — fast follow-up nudges a sooner response in a competitive market.
