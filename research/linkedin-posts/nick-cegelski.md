## 1. The "Heard the Name Tossed Around" cold-call opener
**Posted:** ~2024 · [permalink](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nick-cegelski_heres-a-cold-call-opener-that-makes-them-ugcPost-7340789810430660608-JLrE)

An opener that makes them forget it's a cold call. 3 steps:
1. **1st thing you say** = any commonality between them & your current customers.
2. **2nd thing** = your first name + company.
3. **3rd thing** = "Heard the name tossed around?"

> "Danielle, we work with a few other Home Cares in Eastern PA. It's Nick from Vitable… Heard the name tossed around?"

The point: unless you work at Microsoft, they won't have heard your name, that's not the goal. By mentioning you work with their **peers**, you stop being a random cold caller and position yourself as someone in (or adjacent to) their network, much harder to dismiss. *(This is the same opener detailed in the 30MPC framework, good corroboration.)*

## 2. Kade Hinkle's unconventional opener (why creative beats copied)
**Posted:** ~2026 · [permalink](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nick-cegelski_i-love-kade-hinkles-unconventional-cold-ugcPost-7451042253596278784-oztd)

3 things that make it work:
1. **A one-word opener**: literally just the prospect's name ("Alex?"). Based on how they respond (guarded, friendly, curious), he calibrates pacing & tonality to match.
2. **"I'm calling to book a meeting with you"**: the 2nd thing he says; 99% respond "About what?", which opens the door to the pitch.
3. **It's different**: he invented his own strategy rather than copying a permission-based opener.

> "You can't be perceived to be better until you're first perceived to be different."

## 3. Demo tactic: ask the prospect to "hold" your product
**Posted:** ~late 2025 · [permalink](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nick-cegelski_i-was-forced-to-dress-as-buddy-the-elf-and-ugcPost-7406495762093813760-_DqG)

On a demo, ask the prospect to envision *using* the product instead of asking "any questions?":
- "If you were looking at this dashboard, which KPIs would you drill into first?"
- "Where would you integrate this into new-hire onboarding?"
- "What data here would you want to show your COO?"

Getting them to picture "holding" the product makes them imagine life after the purchase and drives far better engagement than a passive "any questions?"
