## 1. "An object at rest stays at rest": what actually breaks buyer inertia
**Posted:** ~2025 · [permalink](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/beccholland-flipthescript_an-object-at-rest-stays-at-rest-a-buyer-ugcPost-7391862644808450048-HXSu)

A buyer at rest stays at rest unless acted on by an outside force. Before your email, the buyer was at rest, and even if they *have* pain and describe it, they were at rest before you reached out, so they're likely a "tire kicker" evaluating what they'd hypothetically do *if* the problem ever got big enough.

So when you write "We work with teams who struggle with ____," hoping they see themselves in it, keep two things in mind:
- If they **do** see themselves in it, by definition they're running into it, **but it's not big enough to solve for.**
- The force that breaks inertia **isn't your cold email.** It's **you uncovering something they didn't know but now can't unsee** (a missed diagnosis / misdiagnosis).

> "THAT'S the real force that breaks inertia."

*Ties directly to the synthesis: relevance gets you in the door, but the human job is finding the unseen problem, exactly the work AI-at-scale can't fully do.*
