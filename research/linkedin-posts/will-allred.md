## 1. The "Founder Card" outbound play
**Posted:** ~2025 · [permalink](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/williamallred_heres-an-outbound-play-that-i-call-the-share-7389558256089858048-YqW9)

A pattern-interrupt play for when you're bringing a new product to market, hitting a new persona/geography, or your outbound just isn't working: **shift mindset, be transparent, seek feedback**: reach out *like a founder* who's getting them involved rather than trying to convert.

Example email:
> **Subj: Feedback: CS Idea**
> Hey Will, given your history leading CS teams at \_\_\_, \_\_\_, and now \_\_\_, I thought you'd be the perfect person to give me feedback. Normally I'd reach out to sales leaders about our outbound agent Ora. But our team was talking yesterday, our agent's constant research could give CSMs more timely outbound than the standard "checking in… wanna do a QBR and renew." I imagine we're missing some things. Down to give some feedback?

The move: you don't pretend you're going to crush it; you get them involved > trying to convert. Works as a reset when outbound isn't getting replies.

## 2. Cold email personalization doesn't always "make sense"
**Posted:** ~2026 · [permalink](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/williamallred_cold-email-personalization-doesnt-always-share-7471028552386285568-3v_Y)

> *Note: this is a Lavender (AI-email) founder arguing personalization isn't always worth it, directly relevant to the human-vs-AI tension in [`../synthesis.md`](../synthesis.md).*

It depends on **target audience, price point, and deal-cycle length.**
- **Enterprise / large deal** (e.g. selling EHR software to Walmart/CVS): make it 1:1, the deal size and stickiness justify figuring out a clearly-written, tailored email.
- **SMB / low deal size** (independent practices): you *could* name-drop and personalize hypotheses about their workflow pains… but "is the juice worth the squeeze?" Having tested it, **the answer seems to be no.** You're better off investing heavily in **product marketing**: knowing who closes, who's involved, what they care about, how they talk about it, their doubts and motivations.

Then it's about crafting the message: breaking patterns, making it easy to understand and easy to respond to.

## 3. 6 cold-email writing exercises (clarity drills)

Six workshops for intentional practice → better email results:

2. **Personalization Process Design**: the only way to scale personalization
